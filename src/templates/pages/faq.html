❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about Sonora and scale detection

🎯 Getting Started

What is Sonora?

▼
Sonora is an AI-powered scale detection platform that helps musicians identify the scales they play on the Bansuri (Indian flute). Using advanced audio analysis and machine learning, Sonora recognizes musical scales in real-time and provides detailed insights about your performance.

How do I start using Sonora?

▼
Getting started is simple:
  • Visit the Detector page
  • Choose either Live Recording or Upload a File
  • Play your flute or select your audio file
  • Sonora will analyze and show you the detected scale with confidence score
  • Your detection is automatically saved to your profile

Is Sonora free to use?

▼
Yes! Sonora is completely free to use. All scale detection, learning resources, and profile features are available at no cost. We believe music education should be accessible to everyone.

🎤 Detection & Accuracy

What instruments does Sonora support?

▼
Currently, Sonora is optimized for the Bansuri (Indian bamboo flute) and detects scales from the 10 classical Indian Thaats. We're working on expanding support for other instruments and musical traditions in future versions.

How accurate is the scale detection?

▼
Sonora achieves 99% accuracy under ideal conditions. Accuracy can vary based on:
  • Audio Quality: Cleaner recordings produce better results
  • Background Noise: Quiet environments improve accuracy
  • Playing Clarity: Clear, distinct notes are easier to identify
  • Scale Complexity: Some scales are easier to detect than others

Why did Sonora not detect my scale correctly?

▼
This can happen for several reasons:
  • Too much background noise during recording
  • Rapid scale transitions that confuse the algorithm
  • Playing unclear or wavering notes
  • Very short recordings (try recording for at least 10 seconds)
Tip: Try recording in a quiet room with clear note separation.

What do the confidence scores mean?

▼
The confidence score (0-100%) indicates how certain Sonora is about the detected scale:
  • 90-100%: Very confident - clear scale identification
  • 70-89%: Confident - good identification with minor ambiguity
  • 50-69%: Moderate - scale uncertain, consider the alternatives
  • Below 50%: Low confidence - try recording again

📚 Learning & Resources

Where can I learn about scales?

▼
Sonora offers comprehensive learning resources:
  • Scales Library: Detailed information about all 10 Thaats
  • Fingering Guide: Step-by-step instructions for playing each note
  • Tutorial Videos: From beginner to advanced levels
  • Audio Examples: Reference recordings for ear training

How long will it take to master the scales?

▼
This varies by individual dedication and prior experience. Generally:
  • Basic proficiency: 2-3 months with regular practice
  • Intermediate level: 6-12 months
  • Advanced mastery: 2-3 years of consistent practice
Sonora helps accelerate your learning by providing instant feedback and structured resources.

🔒 Privacy & Security

Is my data secure?

▼
Yes! We take security very seriously:
  • All audio files are processed securely
  • Your detection history is stored privately
  • We never share your data with third parties
  • Your profile and settings are protected

Will my recordings be stored?

▼
Audio files are processed for analysis but not permanently stored. Only the detection results (scale, confidence, timestamp) are saved to your profile. You can delete your history anytime.

Do I need an account?

▼
No account is required! You can start detecting scales immediately. Your profile is automatically created, and your detection history is saved. If you want to save your progress across devices, please create an account for better experience.
