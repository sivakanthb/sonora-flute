Indian Classical Scales

Explore the 10 Thaats - the foundational framework of Indian classical music. Each Thaat contains multiple Ragas that express different emotions and moods.

🎶 Bhairav

Beginner Friendly

The regal Thaat, often associated with morning ragas. Characterized by a somber, serious mood.

Sa re Ma Pa Dha
Mood: Serious, Devotional
Best Ragas: Bhairav, Ahir Bhairav
Time: Early Morning

🌟 Kalyan

Beginner Friendly

The auspicious Thaat with a sharp fourth note, creating a bright and celebratory character.

Sa Re Ga Ma# Pa Dha Ni
Mood: Joyful, Auspicious
Best Ragas: Yaman, Kamod
Time: Evening

💫 Khamaj

Intermediate

Known for its folk-like, relaxed nature. Often used in light classical and folk music.

Sa Re Ga Ma Pa Dha Ni♭
Mood: Relaxed, Folksy
Best Ragas: Jog, Bageshri
Time: Late Evening

🌙 Asavari

A minor-like scale with a melancholic and introspective quality. Often evokes sadness or longing.

Sa Re♭ Ga Ma Pa Dha♭ Ni♭
Mood: Sad, Introspective
Best Ragas: Asavari, Adana
Time: Late Night

☀️ Bilaval

The natural major scale without any sharp or flat notes. Bright and clear character.

Sa Re Ga Ma Pa Dha Ni
Mood: Pure, Natural
Best Ragas: Bhoop, Deshkar
Time: Daytime

✨ Marva

An exotic scale with no natural fourth and seventh notes. Often carries mystery and yearning.

Sa Re♭ Ga Ma# Pa Dha#
Mood: Mysterious, Yearning
Best Ragas: Marva, Marva Behag
Time: Night

🎭 Purvi

Similar to Marva but with a different hierarchy. Known for passion and fury in classical contexts.

Sa Re♭ Ga Ma# Pa Dha Ni♭
Mood: Passionate, Energetic
Best Ragas: Purvi, Malkauns
Time: Late Night

🔔 Dheerashankar

A unique scale with distinct note patterns. Elegant and contemplative in character.

Sa Re♭ Ga Ma# Pa Dha# Ni♭
Mood: Elegant, Contemplative
Best Ragas: Malkauns, Rampak
Time: Late Night

🌸 Todi

A deeply devotional scale with a mournful quality. Often used in religious and spiritual contexts.

Sa Re♭ Ga Ma Pa Dha♭ Ni♭
Mood: Devotional, Mournful
Best Ragas: Todi, Bageshri
Time: Last quarter of night

🎨 Kafi

A soft, tender scale. Often used for romantic and gentle ragas with a swaying quality.

Sa Re♭ Ga Ma Pa Dha♭ Ni
Mood: Tender, Romantic
Best Ragas: Bageshri, Patdeep
Time: Monsoon

Understanding the Notes

Sa - Shuddh (Natural)
Root note
Re - Komal (Flat) or Shuddh
2nd note
Ga - Komal or Shuddh
3rd note
Ma - Shuddh or Tivra (Sharp)
4th note
Pa - Shuddh (Always natural)
5th note
Dha - Komal or Shuddh
6th note
Ni - Komal or Shuddh
7th note
