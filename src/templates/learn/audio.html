The Seven Notes (Saptaswara)

Sa Re Ga Ma Pa Dha Ni — The Foundation

Indian classical music is built on seven fundamental notes called Saptaswara. Each note carries a distinct emotional quality and together they form the octave that underpins every raag and melody.

Sa (Shadaj) Re (Rishabh) Ga (Gandhar) Ma (Madhyam) Pa (Pancham) Dha (Dhaivat) Ni (Nishad)

Listen to the recordings above to hear how these notes come alive on the bansuri. The bass bansuri clip demonstrates a slow alap where individual notes are explored with meend (gliding) and andolan (gentle oscillation).

Tips for Ear Training

  • Start with single notes: Listen to each note multiple times to internalize its unique quality and pitch
  • Compare traditions: Notice how the same raag framework sounds different on Hindustani bansuri vs Carnatic flute
  • Identify moods: Try to feel and describe the emotional essence of each raag before reading about it
  • Regular practice: Listen to these examples daily for at least 15-20 minutes for effective learning
  • Active listening: Close your eyes and focus entirely on the music — don't multitask while listening
  • Sing along: After listening, try to hum or sing the phrases to reinforce your learning
