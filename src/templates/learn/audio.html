The Seven Notes (Saptasuvar)

Sa (Shadaj)

The root note - the foundation of all Indian music. Listen to its pure, resonant quality.

Pure 🎯 1.5 sec

Re (Rishabh)

The second note - a bright and natural progression from Sa.

Natural 🎯 1.5 sec

Ga (Gandhar)

The third note - adds character and warmth to musical phrases.

Warm 🎯 1.5 sec

Ma (Madhyam)

The fourth note - the middle pivot that divides the octave symmetrically.

Pivot 🎯 1.5 sec

Pa (Pancham)

The fifth note - the perfect consonant note that appears in most raags.

Consonant 🎯 1.5 sec

Dha (Dhaivat)

The sixth note - adds expressiveness to ascending phrases.

Expressive 🎯 1.5 sec

Ni (Nishad)

The seventh note - the highest in the octave, often used as a leading tone.

High 🎯 1.5 sec

Complete Scale Sequences (Sargam)

Practice These Ascending & Descending Scales

Major Scale (Bilaval) Easy

Sa Re Ga Ma Pa Dha Ni Sa (ascending) then reverse (descending). The natural major scale.

Minor Scale (Asavari) Easy

Sa Re♭ Ga Ma Pa Dha♭ Ni♭ Sa. The minor-like scale with melancholic quality.

Kalyan Scale Medium

Sa Re Ga Ma♯ Pa Dha Ni Sa. Known for brightness and auspicious qualities.

Khamaj Scale Medium

Sa Re Ga Ma Pa Dha Ni♭ Sa. Folk-like and relaxed in character.

Bhairav Scale Medium

Sa Re♭ Ga Ma Pa Dha Ni♭ Sa. Regal and serious in mood.

Marva Scale Hard

Sa Re♭ Ga Ma♯ Pa Dha♯ Ni♭ Sa. Exotic scale with mysterious character.

🎯 Tips for Ear Training

  • Start with single notes: Listen to each note multiple times to internalize its unique quality and pitch
  • Scale sequences: Progress from simple scales to more complex ones as your ear develops
  • Compare variations: Listen to the same raag by different masters to understand its core identity despite variations
  • Identify moods: Try to feel and describe the emotional essence of each raag before reading about it
  • Regular practice: Listen to these examples daily for at least 15-20 minutes for effective learning
  • Active listening: Close your eyes and focus entirely on the music. Don't multitask while listening
  • Learn the rules: Understanding raag theory (which notes are essential, ornamental, etc.) enhances your listening perception
  • Sing along: After listening, try to hum or sing the phrases to reinforce your learning
