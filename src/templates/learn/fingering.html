Bansuri Fingering Guide

Master the fingering patterns and techniques for playing the seven notes on the Bansuri with confidence and clarity.

Hand Position & Grip

Proper Bansuri Holding Technique

🎵

Bansuri should be held at approximately 45° angle from horizontal

Left Hand Position
• Place thumb under the flute
• Index, middle, and ring fingers cover holes 1-3
• Keep wrist relaxed and curved
• Fingers should be slightly curved, not flat
Right Hand Position
• Place thumb under the flute
• Index, middle, ring fingers cover holes 4-6
• Pinky covers the lowest hole
• Maintain steady, controlled positioning
Breathing Technique
• Breathe from the diaphragm
• Keep embouchure relaxed but firm
• Air should flow smoothly and constantly
• Practice long notes to develop breath control

The Seven Notes (Saptasuvar)

Sa (Shadaj)
Holes: All Closed ●●●●●●●

Standard: All holes covered
Tips:
• The root note - play it clearly
• Use steady breath
• Foundation for all other notes
Re (Rishabh)
First Hole: Open ○●●●●●●

Only top hole uncovered
Tips:
• Slightly increase air speed
• Keep other holes sealed
• Natural movement from Sa
Ga (Gandhar)
Two Holes: Open ○○●●●●●

First two holes uncovered
Tips:
• Cover holes just enough
• Avoid leaking air
• Maintain consistent pressure
Ma (Madhyam)
Three Holes: Open ○○○●●●●

First three holes uncovered
Tips:
• Clear transition from Ga
• Slight increase in breath
• Middle pivot note
Pa (Pancham)
Four Holes: Open ○○○○●●●

First four holes uncovered
Tips:
• Perfect fifth interval
• Very stable note to play
• Important in many ragas
Dha (Dhaivat)
Five Holes: Open ○○○○○●●

First five holes uncovered
Tips:
• Maintain air steadiness
• Keep bottom holes sealed
• Flowing transition to Ni
Ni (Nishad)
Six Holes: Open ○○○○○○●

Only last hole covered
Tips:
• Nearly all holes open
• High note - be gentle
• Must be clear and pure

Classical Ornaments (Alaankaar)

🌊 Meend (Glide/Bend)
A smooth glide from one note to another. Achieved by gradually opening or closing holes to create a sliding effect. The most characteristic ornament in Indian flute music.
✨ Gamak (Rapid Oscillation)
Quick oscillation between adjacent notes or between a note and its variations. Created by rapid finger movements and breath control.
🎯 Khat (Staccato/Subtle Cut)
A subtle cut or pause in sound, not a true pause but a slight reduction in tone. Adds rhythmic interest and articulation to phrases.
🔄 Murkhi (Rapid Descending Phrase)
A quick series of notes descending rapidly, often used as a flourish or filler between main phrases. Requires quick finger work.
🌀 Shake (Tremolo)
A trembling effect created by rapidly varying air pressure or subtle finger movements. Adds vibrato-like quality to notes.
➡️ Andolan (Sway)
A gentle swaying of a note, achieved by slight finger movements that don't fully close or open holes. Creates a wobbling pitch effect.

Practice Guidelines for Mastery

  • Start Slow: Practice each note individually at a slow pace before moving to combinations
  • Build Scales: Practice ascending and descending scales (Sargam) daily
  • Clean Holes: Keep your Bansuri holes clean and free of blockages
  • Air Control: Develop diaphragmic breathing for sustained, smooth notes
  • Ear Training: Listen to master musicians and try to replicate their techniques
  • Consistency: Practice at least 30 minutes daily for noticeable improvement
  • Patience: Meend and ornaments require months to master - don't rush
  • Record Yourself: Use your phone to record and analyze your practice sessions
